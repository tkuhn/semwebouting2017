Diverse Politicians Make More Successful Countries

If you are a responsible citizen, you better elect a diverse set of politicians. It turns out, whether you support diversity for ideological reasons or not (is it the right thing to do?), it is in fact the smart thing to do.

But let's have a look at the data. As a first example, let's pick the Netherlands. A first important dimension of diversity is the gender distribution of politicians. The following chart shows the gender distribution of candidates of the Dutch general election 2017:

The Netherlands are clearly still quite a bit away from an equal distribution, but women make up a considerable share of Dutch politicians. Other countries like Yemen clearly show a much less equal distribution and therefore lower degree of diversity:

There are many writers and trade unionists among Dutch politicians. Now, let's turn to Yemen again and look at the side-occupations of 338 politicians from Yemen:

Instead of writers and trade unionist, we see now diplomats and military personnel. Or in other words, instead of independent thinkers and politicians with a close connection to workers, we have members of the elite in diplomacy and army.

So, we have seen that countries like the Netherlands are quite different in terms of diversity in politics. But does it matter? Except for ideological reasons?

It does. We can see that when looking at the Human Development Index, for example:

Diverse countries like the Netherlands fare much better in development than non-diverse countries like Yemen.

But it's not just development that suffers from a lack of diversity in government. Countries with non-diverse politics also tend to be more unequal in general, as the Gini Coefficient shows:

The bottom line is, whether you support diversity for ideological reasons or not, countries with diverse politicians are more successful, more developed, and suffer less from inequality. If your country wants to be successful in the future, the data suggest, it should better make sure to pay attention to the diversity of its politicians.
